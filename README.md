# Trader Behavior vs Market Sentiment Analysis

This project analyzes how trader behavior changes across different market sentiment regimes (Fear, Extreme Fear, Neutral, Greed, Extreme Greed) using historical trading data and the Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index.

## Objective
To explore the relationship between market sentiment and:
- Trading activity
- Capital deployment
- Price behavior

## Datasets
- Historical Trader Data (Hyperliquid)
- Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index

## Key Insights
- Trading activity is highest during Extreme Fear, indicating panic-driven behavior.
- Capital deployment increases during Fear and Extreme Fear phases.
- Activity and exposure drop during Extreme Greed.
- Average traded prices are lower during Greed phases, suggesting accumulation behavior.

## Tools Used
- Python
- Pandas
- Matplotlib
- Jupyter Notebook

## Outcome
The analysis highlights the importance of incorporating market sentiment into risk management and trading strategy design.
